# Project Overview

This project analyzes adoption and usage of a transaction platform among external business partners.

The main purpose is to provide leadership with clear visibility into:

- current adoption levels
- participation depth
- concentration risk
- untapped growth opportunity
- behavioral differences across partner maturity groups

The analysis is cumulative in nature for partner and user counts. This means the measures are designed to answer "as of this point in time" questions rather than "within this period only" questions.

For activity-based measures such as transaction counts, time-based trends and period analysis are used where appropriate.

# Business Questions

This project was designed to answer the following questions:

1. How many active partners exist as of the selected date?
2. How many of those partners have adopted the platform?
3. What percentage of active partners are users?
4. How many active partners remain untapped?
5. Of current users, how many actively transact on the platform?
6. Is usage concentrated among a small subset of users?
7. Which maturity groups are adopting the platform at the highest rate?
8. How is adoption evolving over time?

## Audience

- Executives: high-level adoption KPIs and strategic trends
- Operational leaders: adoption patterns across organizational segments
- Business teams: user concentration, participation, and opportunity tracking

## Analytical Design Principles

- keep executive KPIs simple and decision-oriented
- separate stock measures from flow measures
- avoid misleading time intelligence on cumulative entity counts
- use ratios to communicate adoption performance clearly
- highlight both opportunity and concentration risk
